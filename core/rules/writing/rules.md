# CANONICAL WRITING RULES — Liem OS

These rules govern all copywriting, social media distribution, documentation, newsletters, and developer advocacy content in Liem OS.

---

## 1. Input-Driven Copywriting & Density

To maintain authenticity and capture the reader's attention:

- **Strict Source Mapping**: All copy must render source inputs (walkthroughs, briefs, codebase structures). Do not fabricate claims or statistics that do not exist.
- **Value Density**: Maximize information per sentence. Eliminate fluff phrases, excessive adjectives, and repetitive conclusions.
- **Micro-Spacing**:
  - Keep paragraphs short (maximum 2-3 sentences).
  - Use bullet points, bold key terms, and lists to make content easily scannable by busy developers.

---

## 2. Voice, Tone, and Banned AI Clichés

Write in a direct, confident, and professional developer advocate voice. Avoid typical artificial intelligence speech patterns:

### Banned AI Vocabulary
Do not use these words or phrases under any circumstances. They immediately identify the content as generic AI generation:
- *delve*, *testament*, *unlock*, *seamless*, *moreover*, *furthermore*, *realm*, *tapestry*, *demystify*, *elevate*, *holistic*, *revolutionize*, *cutting-edge*.

### Sentence Guidelines
- Use the **active voice** (e.g. "We optimized the database query," not "The database query was optimized by us").
- Keep explanations simple. Speak directly to the developer's experience and concerns.

---

## 3. Platform Distribution Standards

Format copy specifically for the target medium:

### X/Twitter Threads
- **First Tweet (The Hook)**: Must state the core value proposition, problem, and visual attachment plan clearly.
- **Spacing**: Use ample line breaks between sentences. Do not crowd the 280-character limit.
- **Hashtags**: Avoid hashtag stuffing. Use at most one highly relevant hashtag or omit them entirely.

### LinkedIn Articles/Posts
- **Insight-First**: Start with a professional lesson learned, a migration trade-off, or a technical architectural decision.
- **CTA**: Keep the call-to-action conversational. Invite opinions in the comments section.

### Newsletters
- **Narrative Story**: Use an editorial, narrative voice to explain a problem-solving journey.
- **Structure**: Break down content into: *The Context*, *The Deep Dive (with code)*, and *The Verdict*.
